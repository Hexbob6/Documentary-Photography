Sam WalkerExperiments in Photo-Elasticity.

This photo essay is an exploration of the ways that inherently beautiful, yet ordinarily unseen, scientific phenomena can be revealed and documented, through the medium of photography. The topic of Photo-Elasticity has been the focal technique by which this idea has been demonstrated. It describes the optical properties of a material whereby typically invisible, internal structural stresses can be revealed through the use of polarised light. The images were intentionally captured under contrasting compositions. On one hand, photos such as Cassette and Fluted aim to pay homage to the scientific experiment, where subjects are captured in their entirety to document the purely technical process or phenomena. Conversely, subsequent images are captured using more creative arrangements and camera positions, and the use of a macro-lens has been used to highlight areas of particular visual detail. The images have been grouped together both by subject matter, but also by colour. Subjects that are displayed in their entirety are placed before the more experimental compositions, a metaphor for the way that science has progressed and ultimately transcended the boundaries of art. The work of pioneers from history has been hugely influential during in the development of this project. The motivations of both scientific and theoretical photographers such as Harold Edgerton and Laszlo Moholy-Nagy, alongside more contemporary creative photographers such as Susan Derges, Garry Fabian Miller and Adam Fuss, have provided a substantial foundation of ideas to build the project upon.
